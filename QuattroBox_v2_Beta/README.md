This is the QuattroBox v2 beta, a complete evolution of the original QuattroBox project.
It has been fully redesigned from scratch, bringing several improvements while keeping as many hardware components from version 1 as possible.

##

### What’s New in v2:

- Filament dryer with AC heater

- Exhaust fan to remove humid air

- Dedicated 4A power supply (optional, for those who prefer not to use the printer’s power)

- More compact extruder using a NEMA17 23mm (pancake) motor — other motor sizes will require modifications

- Hub with integrated Sync

- New Hub with Sync PSF

- Hinged lid

- Simplified assembly: all electronics are assembled outside the enclosure first, then installed into the case

- Cable management clips

- Optimized for a 180mm footprint with up to 142mm height

- Dedicated space for an RFID reader (still in development)

### Important Notes

The use of heaters and the dedicated power supply is not mandatory. However, the structural printed parts are still required, as the extruders and hub are mounted to them.

The recommended MCU is the Chameleon X5, as it is the only one with fan and thermistor connections, and it makes sensor wiring easier (all connectors are JST 2.54).

The MMB works perfectly for those who do not plan to use the heaters.

The Hub with Sync PSF is the recommended option and works perfectly with the latest version of Happy Hare.
